Surveillance Station FPS Utilization

NVR spec sheet (H.265 figures)

Presets pulled from current Synology product spec sheets (RS Plus / XS line + HD6500, verified Jul 2026). Every value stays editable. Each resolution is an independent FPS pool; a mixed deployment spends a fraction of each pool and the fractions must total ≤ 100%.

Recording settings & per-camera sizing applied to every site

Enter each class's per-camera bitrate (from the camera's stream settings or NVR Selector) — ingest Mbps uses it directly, and TB/camera is derived from Mbps × recording hrs/day × retention days using the same unit conversion as Synology's NVR Selector, so totals match it exactly. Sizing follows the “Record as” remap, so 4K cameras recorded as 5MP consume the 5MP bitrate. Leave bitrates blank to skip sizing.

Sites — camera counts by resolution class

Per-site columns assume one NVR of this model at each site. Bandwidth is camera ingest to the recorder; the NIC badge flags when a single 1 GbE link won't carry it (>70% = plan for 10 GbE, >100% = required). The fleet row models all sites recording to one central unit.

Single central NVR — all sites combined

Method: utilization = Σ (cameras × recording FPS ÷ total FPS pool at that resolution). Spec figures assume continuous recording on a fully populated unit with RTSP; SRTP drops capacity to ~70% of stated figures, and analytics, playback, and other DSM workloads reduce real-world headroom — treat >70% as the planning ceiling.
